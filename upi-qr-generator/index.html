One QR, every UPI app

The QR generated here encodes a standard NPCI upi://pay link carrying your virtual payment address (UPI ID), your payee name, and optionally a fixed amount and note. Any UPI app — Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM, or your bank's own app — scans it and opens the payment screen addressed to you. Print it at your counter, paste it into a WhatsApp chat, drop the PNG into your email signature, or let the invoice generator place it on your PDFs with the exact balance pre-filled.

Fixed amount or open amount?

  • Fixed amount — the payment screen opens with the figure locked in. Best for invoices and price-tagged goods: no typos, no “oops, I sent ₹100 instead of ₹1,000”.
  • Open amount — leave the amount blank and the payer types what they owe. Right for shop counters, tip jars and donations where the figure varies.

Is a payment QR safe to share?

Yes — and it's worth understanding why. A UPI collect/pay QR can only initiate a payment to you; scanning it never exposes your bank account or lets anyone pull money out. The classic UPI scams work the other way around: a fraudster asks you to scan their QR or approve a collect request “to receive money”. Receiving money via UPI never requires scanning anything or entering your PIN. Share your payment QR freely; guard your PIN absolutely.

Frequently asked questions

Which apps can scan this QR?

All UPI apps: Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM, Amazon Pay, WhatsApp Pay and bank apps. The QR uses the standard NPCI upi://pay format.

Can someone steal money using my QR?

No — a payment QR only addresses payments to you. Money never leaves your account without you entering your UPI PIN in your own app.

Can I fix the amount?

Yes — enter an amount and the payer's app opens pre-filled and locked. Leave blank for an open-amount QR.

Can I print the QR on invoices?

Yes — add your UPI ID in any document generator on this site and the QR is rendered onto the PDF automatically with the balance due as the amount.
